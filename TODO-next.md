## Guiding principle тАФ orientation-(in)dependence splits Calculator and Simulator

**The architectural split follows the physics.** SHG quantities divide cleanly by
whether they depend on the crystal's orientation in the lab frame:

- **Orientation-*independent*, crystal frame** тАФ the symmetry-allowed tensor form
  and the induced crystal-frame response. Pure point-group algebra; no beam
  direction or sample angles enter. тЖТ **Calculator** (Tensor Components, Induced
  Response).
- **Orientation-*dependent*, lab frame** тАФ the source terms projected into the lab
  frame and the polarimetry observables, which depend on cut (**k**), tilt
  (╧Ж_x, ╧Ж_y) and azimuth (╧И). тЖТ **Simulator**.

**Why it matters.** Group, tensor type, time-reversal and setting form a *shared
group context*; today that context is rebuilt separately on each page rather than
shared, and the orientation-(in)dependence line is blurred at the seam (the
Calculator's Source Terms tab). Several open items are corollaries of this one
principle and are best read together:

- **A1** тАФ the Calculator shows orientation-*dependent* source terms although no
  angles can be set there (the seam drawn on the wrong side).
- **B5 / B8** тАФ the lab-frame direction / **k** selector is duplicated and
  differently named instead of being one shared, lab-frame control.
- **B2.4** тАФ the Simulator lacks the setting selector that belongs to the shared
  group context.
- **B3 / B27** тАФ the group-identity header is the natural home of that shared
  context and should be reused, not duplicated.

**Design implication (not a rebuild).** Keep the four areas. Treat the group
context (header: group, type, time-reversal, setting тАФ B27) as persistent and
shared; let the Calculator own the crystal-frame, orientation-free view and the
Simulator own the lab-frame, orientation-dependent view, with the **Source Terms as
the explicit hand-off**: the Calculator shows the source term **frozen at the cut
orientation** (no free angles тАФ A1), and the Simulator **sweeps** it through tilt
(╧Ж_x, ╧Ж_y) and azimuth (╧И) for the polarimetry (B16). So "Source Terms" is not a
contradiction of "Calculator = orientation-free"; it is the single frozen value at
the cut, with the swept family living in the Simulator.

## 1. Crystallographic correctness & conventions

_The physics-correctness backbone тАФ which groups have which settings, and how directions/axes are defined._

### B1 тАФ Alternate settings missing for colorless (Type I) and grey (Type II) groups

**Status:** Derivation / verification pending тАФ settings enumeration & mechanism count (reconcile with B22) before UI work.

**Area:** Feature 3 (Alternate Point Group Settings via similarity transforms `G' = S┬╖G┬╖SтБ╗┬╣`)
**Severity:** MediumтАУHigh тАФ scope/completeness gap with crystallographic-correctness impact *(provisional)*
**Provenance:** Previously discussed, then dropped. Re-captured here.

#### Problem

Feature 3 has so far been framed around the magnetic (blackтАУwhite) groups. But
colorless and grey groups **also** admit more than one standard setting, and the
app currently exposes only one. The extra settings arise from two geometrically
**independent** mechanisms:

**Mechanism 1 тАФ Monoclinic: Birss vs. ITC axis choice.**
The app uses the Birss first-setting convention (unique axis тИе c, i.e. **zтАСunique**).
ITC/Nye use the second setting (**bтАСunique**). The two are related by an axis
permutation `S` that relabels the free tensor components. This deviation applies
to *every* monoclinic class, not only the magnetic ones:

- colorless: `2`, `m`, `2/m`
- grey: `21'`, `m1'`, `2/m1'`

(The b-unique тЖФ c-unique ambiguity for `2/m` is explicitly a recognized
two-convention case; standard = b, Birss = c.)

**Mechanism 2 тАФ Non-monoclinic: ITC orientation ambiguity.**
Several classes appear in ITC under **two orientations** differing by a rotation
about the principal axis; the abstract group is identical, but the tensor *form*
differs because the free components are referred to the crystal axes. Confirmed
complete set (ITC Vol. A, hexagonal-axes description):

| Point group | Settings | Interconversion |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| `тИТ42m` (DтВВd) | `тИТ42m` / `тИТ4m2` | 45┬░ about z |
| `тИТ6m2` (DтВГтВХ) | `тИТ6m2` / `тИТ62m` | 30┬░ about z |
| `32` (DтВГ) | `321` / `312` | 30┬░ about z |
| `3m` (CтВГс╡е) | `3m1` / `31m` | 30┬░ about z |
| `тИТ3m` (DтВГd) | `тИТ3m1` / `тИТ31m` | 30┬░ about z |

Each of these тАФ **plus its grey (`тАж1'`) derivative** тАФ needs both orientations
represented.

> Source: ITC Vol. A ┬з3.2 (point groups and crystal classes). The five classes
> above are exactly the ones ITC lists with two orientations; the tetragonal
> pair turns by 45┬░, the trigonal/hexagonal pairs by 30┬░.

#### Why it matters (not "just labelling")

Group-theoretically `тИТ42m` and `тИТ4m2` differ only in axis labelling. But the
Birss App expresses the SHG d-tensor (and rank-4 tensors) **relative to the
crystal axes**, so the explicit component pattern differs between the two
settings. For a user reading off non-zero components, presenting only one
orientation is silently incomplete.

#### Open question тАФ counting

We previously fixed the maximum at **3 settings per group** with two
mutually-exclusive mechanisms (A, B). Mechanism 2 above (principal-axis rotation)
is geometrically distinct from the monoclinic axis-choice (Mechanism 1). Need to
confirm:

- Is Mechanism 2 already subsumed by the established A/B framework, or is it a
  genuinely **third** independent mechanism?
- The affected crystal systems are **disjoint** (monoclinic vs.
  tetragonal/trigonal/hexagonal), so Mechanisms 1 and 2 should never co-occur in
  a single group тЖТ the **max-of-3 bound likely still holds**, but this should be
  re-derived explicitly rather than assumed.

#### Action items

- [ ] Confirm the Mechanism-2 set (table above) against the local ITC Vol. A
      copy; correct/extend if anything is missing.
- [ ] Extend the settings machinery (`S┬╖G┬╖SтБ╗┬╣`) to **colorless and grey
      monoclinic** classes (the zтАСunique тЖФ bтАСunique permutation `S`).
- [ ] Add the principal-axis rotation transform(s) (45┬░ about z for `тИТ42mтЖФтИТ4m2`;
      30┬░ about z for the trigonal/hexagonal pairs) and apply to all affected
      classes **and their grey derivatives**.
- [ ] Re-derive and update the "max 3 settings per group" note in light of
      Mechanism 2.
- [ ] Transcribe golden fixtures for at least one colorless and one grey
      representative of each new mechanism from cited literature (Birss 1966 /
      Sirotin & Shaskol'skaya), **never** from the app.

#### To verify before asserting in code/docs

- The exact form of `S` (permutation for Mechanism 1; rotation for Mechanism 2)
  and its action on the rank-3 (SHG d) and rank-4 tensors.
- Whether the grey (`тАж1'`) derivatives change which tensors survive (time-reversal
  bookkeeping is separate from the geometric setting question and should not be
  conflated with it).

### B7 тАФ k-direction presets: use only the symmetry directions (Blickrichtungen); remove free [hkl]

**Status:** Decided.

**Area:** Calculator (Source Terms tab) + Simulator тАФ k-direction selection (the shared B5 component)
**Severity:** Medium тАФ correctness of offered choices + simplification *(provisional)*
**Relates to:** B5 (shared k-selector), B1 (trigonal/hexagonal secondaryтЖФtertiary
is setting-dependent), and several v0.7-audit [hkl] items (see note).

**Already shipped (0.7.1) тАФ verified against CHANGELOG + code.** Free `[hkl]` is
already restricted to **cubic only** and hidden on mobile; hex/trig preset labels are
already `[120]`; the psi0 azimuth-zero fix is in. The **remaining scope** is therefore
narrower than the audit note below suggests: the cubic/tetragonal **preset redundancy**
(`PRESETS_BY_SYSTEM`) and the decision whether to drop the cubic free-input entirely.

**Decisions (Thomas).**
1. **Remove the free [hkl] input** entirely тАФ it now exists only for cubic and
   is not worth maintaining.
2. **Do not offer symmetry-equivalent directions** (e.g. [001] and [100] in
   cubic тАФ both тЯи100тЯй).
3. Offer **only the symmetry principal directions (Blickrichtungen)**, one
   representative per тЯитАжтЯй family.

**Symmetry directions per system (representatives тАФ confirmed).**

| System | Primary | Secondary | Tertiary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Triclinic | тАФ | тАФ | тАФ |
| Monoclinic | unique axis: [010] (b-unique) / [001] (Birss, c-unique) | тАФ | тАФ |
| Orthorhombic | [100] | [010] | [001] |
| Tetragonal | [001] | [100] (тЯи100тЯй) | [110] (тЯи110тЯй) |
| Trigonal | [001] | [100] (тЯи100тЯй) | [1╠Д10] / [120] тЙЩ b* (тЯи1╠Д10тЯй) |
| Hexagonal | [001] | [100] (тЯи100тЯй) | [1╠Д10] / [120] тЙЩ b* (тЯи1╠Д10тЯй) |
| Cubic | [100] (тЯи100тЯй) | [111] (тЯи111тЯй) | [110] (тЯи110тЯй) |

**Caveats тАФ triclinic & monoclinic keep their existing (conventional) selection.**
These two systems do **not** have three symmetry directions, so their current
crystal-physics axis presets are **kept**, not replaced. The frame follows the
standard crystal-physics prescription (Hauss├╝hl 1983, based on IRE 1949):
**Z тИе c, Y тИе (c ├Ч a) тИе b\*, X = Y ├Ч Z**.

- **Triclinic:** z тИе c; y тИе (c ├Ч a) тИе b\*; x = y ├Ч z = projection of **a** onto
  the plane тКе c. (No symmetry directions тАФ conventional, not symmetry-fixed.)
- **Monoclinic:** z тИе c (unique axis: тИе the 2-fold / тКе the mirror); x тИе a;
  y тИе b\*. (Exactly one symmetry direction тАФ the unique axis; the rest
  conventional. Here a тКе c, so x тИе a exactly.)
- **Trigonal / Hexagonal:** the secondaryтЖФtertiary assignment and the tertiary
  representative ([1╠Д10] vs [120] / b\*) depend on the setting (321 vs 312, тАж) тЖТ
  ties to **B1**.

**Physics note (triclinic / monoclinic).** The set of *independent and zero*
tensor components does **not** depend on this frame choice, but the *numeric*
component values тАФ and therefore the **orientation of the simulated polarimetry
patterns** тАФ **do**. The convention is thus observable in the Simulator output
and must be stated to users тАФ specifically the in-plane Cartesian anchor.

**Status & provenance (from the earlier "non-orthogonal axes" audit, 2026-06-25).**
The tensor engine is verified correct for triclinic/monoclinic: it works in an
orthonormal Cartesian frame with pure point-group generators (no lattice
parameters, no metric tensor), and the independent/zero-component relations are
**convention-independent** (verified by conjugation over a full in-plane angle
grid; monoclinic `2` and `m` match Birss 1966 in the z-unique/first setting).
**So this is a documentation/UI-transparency item, not an engine bug.** Two
guard-rails from that session:
- **Forward order ZтЖТYтЖТX:** `x тИе a` and `y тИе b*` are *consequences* of `ZтИеc`,
  `YтИе(c├Чa)`, `X=Y├ЧZ` together with `aтКеc` (monoclinic) тАФ not independent
  assumptions. State the derivation in that order (the circular phrasing was
  flagged previously).
- **Anti-regression:** the app uses the **Birss first setting (zтИеc)**, *not* the
  ITC b-unique second setting тАФ do **not** "correct" `xтИеa` to `xтИеc` (a prior
  mistake). Triclinic was verified numerically for a general cell
  (╬▒=80┬░, ╬▓=75┬░, ╬│=70┬░).

**Audit (v0.7.1, found in source).** Free [hkl] input is still present тАФ
`hklInput` state + validation at `CalculatorPage.tsx:35, 42тАУ48`, reset at `:272, :444`.
Presets are not yet reduced to Blickrichtungen: `PRESETS_BY_SYSTEM`
(`MathComponents.tsx:55тАУ62`) gives **Cubic** = [001],[100],[010],[110],[111] (the
first three are equivalent тЯи100тЯй тАФ the redundancy to drop тЖТ [100],[111],[110]) and
**Tetragonal** = [001],[100],[010],[110] ([010] redundant with [100] тЖТ
[001],[100],[110]). Hexagonal/Trigonal ([001],[100],[120]) and Mono/Triclinic
([001],[100],[010]тИеb\*) are already as intended.

**Action items.**
- [ ] Remove the free [hkl] input (parsing, validation, UI).
- [ ] **Orthorhombic тЖТ cubic:** replace per-system presets with the (up to three)
      Blickrichtungen, one representative per тЯитАжтЯй family; no symmetry-equivalent
      duplicates.
- [ ] Label presets crystallographically (e.g. b\* / [1╠Д20] for the hexagonal
      tertiary), consistent with `AxisOrientationInfo`.
- [ ] **Triclinic / monoclinic:** keep the existing conventional-axis presets; do
      **not** force a three-direction scheme. **Verify current
      `AxisOrientationInfo`** тАФ later work may already return content for all
      seven systems, so confirm whether the *in-plane* anchor (x = projection of
      a onto тКеc; yтИеb\*) is actually stated, not just the z-axis; that specific
      anchor was the documented gap. Document the forward-order frame
      (Hauss├╝hl 1983 / IRE 1949: ZтИеc, YтИе(c├Чa)тИеb\*, X=Y├ЧZ).

**Note тАФ interaction with the v0.7-audit [hkl] items.** Removing free [hkl]
retires the "reachable on mobile" and "no malformed-input validation" items and
moots the non-cubic orthonormal-metric concern (cubic was the only place it
remained, and the orthonormal metric is correct there anyway). Azimuth-zero
anchoring then only needs to be defined for the fixed preset directions, not for
arbitrary [hkl].

### B15 тАФ Explorer as an interactive Birss table (tensor lookup up to rank 4)

**Status:** Open decision тАФ scope & placement (larger vision item).

**Area:** Explorer (and/or a new "Tables" section) тАФ general tensor lookup
**Severity:** Feature / vision тАФ larger (roadmap-scale) scope *(exploratory)*
**Relates to:** the `manganite/birss-tables` repo (transcribed tables = golden
fixtures), B1 (grey-group c-tensors), B7/B8 (conventions), the SHG engine.

**Vision (Thomas).** Beyond SHG, make the Explorer an *interactive Birss table*:
look up arbitrary property tensors up to rank 4 for any (magnetic) point group,
the way one uses the printed Birss tables тАФ preserving their **logic and
systematics**. SHG stays the headline application; this is the broader capability.

**The Birss systematics to preserve** (from the `birss-tables` repo тАФ a
two-step, *factored* lookup):
1. **(point group, tensor type) тЖТ symbol class AтАУU.** Tensor type = rank (0тАУ4) ├Ч
   polar/axial ├Ч i-tensor (time-even) / c-tensor (time-odd). Table 4a maps the 32
   classical groups тЖТ class; Table 7 extends this to the magnetic groups (i- and
   c-tensor classes). Generating matrices: Table 3 (classical) / Table 6 (magnetic).
2. **(symbol class, rank) тЖТ tensor form.** Tables 4bтАУ4f give the nonzero
   components, equalities and signs per class, indexed by rank (4b = r0 тАж 4f = r4),
   with Birss's permutation shorthand (`xz(2)`, `xxy(3)`, `xxxz(4)`, тАж).

The elegance: the 32/90 groups are **factored into ~21 classes (AтАУU)**, so forms
are tabulated once per class, not per group. (Grey/Type-II groups are omitted in
Tables 6/7: i-tensor = classical, c-tensor тЙб 0 тАФ directly relevant to **B1**.)

**Feasibility.** The engine already generates tensor forms by projecting onto the
symmetry-invariant subspace; the approach generalizes to any rank тЙд4 and type. So
most of the work is **specifying the tensor type + presenting results in Birss
form/notation**, not new physics. (Verify how rank-parametrized the current
generator is vs. hardcoded to the SHG case.) **Anti-circularity:** the interactive
output is engine-*generated*, so it must be validated against the transcribed
tables in `birss-tables` (golden fixtures) тАФ never the reverse. Those markdown
tables are a ready-made automated-test fixture set.

**Where it lives тАФ proposal (don't overload the Explorer): two entry points, one
engine.**
- **Explorer, per-group:** a lightweight "Tensor forms" tab тАФ pick rank + type тЖТ
  show this group's **symbol class** and its **tensor form**. Group-centric, one
  extra tab, progressive disclosure, no clutter.
