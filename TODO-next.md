## Guiding principle — orientation-(in)dependence splits Calculator and Simulator

**The architectural split follows the physics.** SHG quantities divide cleanly by
whether they depend on the crystal's orientation in the lab frame:

- **Orientation-*independent*, crystal frame** — the symmetry-allowed tensor form
  and the induced crystal-frame response. Pure point-group algebra; no beam
  direction or sample angles enter. → **Calculator** (Tensor Components, Induced
  Response).
- **Orientation-*dependent*, lab frame** — the source terms projected into the lab
  frame and the polarimetry observables, which depend on cut (**k**), tilt
  (φ_x, φ_y) and azimuth (ψ). → **Simulator**.

**Why it matters.** Group, tensor type, time-reversal and setting form a *shared
group context*; today that context is rebuilt separately on each page rather than
shared, and the orientation-(in)dependence line is blurred at the seam (the
Calculator's Source Terms tab). Several open items are corollaries of this one
principle and are best read together:

- **A1** — the Calculator shows orientation-*dependent* source terms although no
  angles can be set there (the seam drawn on the wrong side).
- **B5 / B8** — the lab-frame direction / **k** selector is duplicated and
  differently named instead of being one shared, lab-frame control.
- **B2.4** — the Simulator lacks the setting selector that belongs to the shared
  group context.
- **B3 / B27** — the group-identity header is the natural home of that shared
  context and should be reused, not duplicated.

**Design implication (not a rebuild).** Keep the four areas. Treat the group
context (header: group, type, time-reversal, setting — B27) as persistent and
shared; let the Calculator own the crystal-frame, orientation-free view and the
Simulator own the lab-frame, orientation-dependent view, with the **Source Terms as
the explicit hand-off**: the Calculator shows the source term **frozen at the cut
orientation** (no free angles — A1), and the Simulator **sweeps** it through tilt
(φ_x, φ_y) and azimuth (ψ) for the polarimetry (B16). So "Source Terms" is not a
contradiction of "Calculator = orientation-free"; it is the single frozen value at
the cut, with the swept family living in the Simulator.

## 1. Crystallographic correctness & conventions

_The physics-correctness backbone — which groups have which settings, and how directions/axes are defined._

### B1 — Alternate settings missing for colorless (Type I) and grey (Type II) groups

**Status:** Derivation / verification pending — settings enumeration & mechanism count (reconcile with B22) before UI work.

**Area:** Feature 3 (Alternate Point Group Settings via similarity transforms `G' = S·G·S⁻¹`)
**Severity:** Medium–High — scope/completeness gap with crystallographic-correctness impact *(provisional)*
**Provenance:** Previously discussed, then dropped. Re-captured here.

#### Problem

Feature 3 has so far been framed around the magnetic (black–white) groups. But
colorless and grey groups **also** admit more than one standard setting, and the
app currently exposes only one. The extra settings arise from two geometrically
**independent** mechanisms:

**Mechanism 1 — Monoclinic: Birss vs. ITC axis choice.**
The app uses the Birss first-setting convention (unique axis ∥ c, i.e. **z‑unique**).
ITC/Nye use the second setting (**b‑unique**). The two are related by an axis
permutation `S` that relabels the free tensor components. This deviation applies
to *every* monoclinic class, not only the magnetic ones:

- colorless: `2`, `m`, `2/m`
- grey: `21'`, `m1'`, `2/m1'`

(The b-unique ↔ c-unique ambiguity for `2/m` is explicitly a recognized
two-convention case; standard = b, Birss = c.)

**Mechanism 2 — Non-monoclinic: ITC orientation ambiguity.**
Several classes appear in ITC under **two orientations** differing by a rotation
about the principal axis; the abstract group is identical, but the tensor *form*
differs because the free components are referred to the crystal axes. Confirmed
complete set (ITC Vol. A, hexagonal-axes description):

| Point group | Settings | Interconversion |
|-------------|----------|-----------------|
| `−42m` (D₂d) | `−42m` / `−4m2` | 45° about z |
| `−6m2` (D₃ₕ) | `−6m2` / `−62m` | 30° about z |
| `32` (D₃) | `321` / `312` | 30° about z |
| `3m` (C₃ᵥ) | `3m1` / `31m` | 30° about z |
| `−3m` (D₃d) | `−3m1` / `−31m` | 30° about z |

Each of these — **plus its grey (`…1'`) derivative** — needs both orientations
represented.

> Source: ITC Vol. A §3.2 (point groups and crystal classes). The five classes
> above are exactly the ones ITC lists with two orientations; the tetragonal
> pair turns by 45°, the trigonal/hexagonal pairs by 30°.

#### Why it matters (not "just labelling")

Group-theoretically `−42m` and `−4m2` differ only in axis labelling. But the
Birss App expresses the SHG d-tensor (and rank-4 tensors) **relative to the
crystal axes**, so the explicit component pattern differs between the two
settings. For a user reading off non-zero components, presenting only one
orientation is silently incomplete.

#### Open question — counting

We previously fixed the maximum at **3 settings per group** with two
mutually-exclusive mechanisms (A, B). Mechanism 2 above (principal-axis rotation)
is geometrically distinct from the monoclinic axis-choice (Mechanism 1). Need to
confirm:

- Is Mechanism 2 already subsumed by the established A/B framework, or is it a
  genuinely **third** independent mechanism?
- The affected crystal systems are **disjoint** (monoclinic vs.
  tetragonal/trigonal/hexagonal), so Mechanisms 1 and 2 should never co-occur in
  a single group → the **max-of-3 bound likely still holds**, but this should be
  re-derived explicitly rather than assumed.

#### Action items

- [ ] Confirm the Mechanism-2 set (table above) against the local ITC Vol. A
      copy; correct/extend if anything is missing.
- [ ] Extend the settings machinery (`S·G·S⁻¹`) to **colorless and grey
      monoclinic** classes (the z‑unique ↔ b‑unique permutation `S`).
- [ ] Add the principal-axis rotation transform(s) (45° about z for `−42m↔−4m2`;
      30° about z for the trigonal/hexagonal pairs) and apply to all affected
      classes **and their grey derivatives**.
- [ ] Re-derive and update the "max 3 settings per group" note in light of
      Mechanism 2.
- [ ] Transcribe golden fixtures for at least one colorless and one grey
      representative of each new mechanism from cited literature (Birss 1966 /
      Sirotin & Shaskol'skaya), **never** from the app.

#### To verify before asserting in code/docs

- The exact form of `S` (permutation for Mechanism 1; rotation for Mechanism 2)
  and its action on the rank-3 (SHG d) and rank-4 tensors.
- Whether the grey (`…1'`) derivatives change which tensors survive (time-reversal
  bookkeeping is separate from the geometric setting question and should not be
  conflated with it).

### B7 — k-direction presets: use only the symmetry directions (Blickrichtungen); remove free [hkl]

**Status:** Decided.

**Area:** Calculator (Source Terms tab) + Simulator — k-direction selection (the shared B5 component)
**Severity:** Medium — correctness of offered choices + simplification *(provisional)*
**Relates to:** B5 (shared k-selector), B1 (trigonal/hexagonal secondary↔tertiary
is setting-dependent), and several v0.7-audit [hkl] items (see note).

**Already shipped (0.7.1) — verified against CHANGELOG + code.** Free `[hkl]` is
already restricted to **cubic only** and hidden on mobile; hex/trig preset labels are
already `[120]`; the psi0 azimuth-zero fix is in. The **remaining scope** is therefore
narrower than the audit note below suggests: the cubic/tetragonal **preset redundancy**
(`PRESETS_BY_SYSTEM`) and the decision whether to drop the cubic free-input entirely.

**Decisions (Thomas).**
1. **Remove the free [hkl] input** entirely — it now exists only for cubic and
   is not worth maintaining.
2. **Do not offer symmetry-equivalent directions** (e.g. [001] and [100] in
   cubic — both ⟨100⟩).
3. Offer **only the symmetry principal directions (Blickrichtungen)**, one
   representative per ⟨…⟩ family.

**Symmetry directions per system (representatives — confirmed).**

| System | Primary | Secondary | Tertiary |
|---|---|---|---|
| Triclinic | — | — | — |
| Monoclinic | unique axis: [010] (b-unique) / [001] (Birss, c-unique) | — | — |
| Orthorhombic | [100] | [010] | [001] |
| Tetragonal | [001] | [100] (⟨100⟩) | [110] (⟨110⟩) |
| Trigonal | [001] | [100] (⟨100⟩) | [1̄10] / [120] ≙ b* (⟨1̄10⟩) |
| Hexagonal | [001] | [100] (⟨100⟩) | [1̄10] / [120] ≙ b* (⟨1̄10⟩) |
| Cubic | [100] (⟨100⟩) | [111] (⟨111⟩) | [110] (⟨110⟩) |

**Caveats — triclinic & monoclinic keep their existing (conventional) selection.**
These two systems do **not** have three symmetry directions, so their current
crystal-physics axis presets are **kept**, not replaced. The frame follows the
standard crystal-physics prescription (Haussühl 1983, based on IRE 1949):
**Z ∥ c, Y ∥ (c × a) ∥ b\*, X = Y × Z**.

- **Triclinic:** z ∥ c; y ∥ (c × a) ∥ b\*; x = y × z = projection of **a** onto
  the plane ⊥ c. (No symmetry directions — conventional, not symmetry-fixed.)
- **Monoclinic:** z ∥ c (unique axis: ∥ the 2-fold / ⊥ the mirror); x ∥ a;
  y ∥ b\*. (Exactly one symmetry direction — the unique axis; the rest
  conventional. Here a ⊥ c, so x ∥ a exactly.)
- **Trigonal / Hexagonal:** the secondary↔tertiary assignment and the tertiary
  representative ([1̄10] vs [120] / b\*) depend on the setting (321 vs 312, …) →
  ties to **B1**.

**Physics note (triclinic / monoclinic).** The set of *independent and zero*
tensor components does **not** depend on this frame choice, but the *numeric*
component values — and therefore the **orientation of the simulated polarimetry
patterns** — **do**. The convention is thus observable in the Simulator output
and must be stated to users — specifically the in-plane Cartesian anchor.

**Status & provenance (from the earlier "non-orthogonal axes" audit, 2026-06-25).**
The tensor engine is verified correct for triclinic/monoclinic: it works in an
orthonormal Cartesian frame with pure point-group generators (no lattice
parameters, no metric tensor), and the independent/zero-component relations are
**convention-independent** (verified by conjugation over a full in-plane angle
grid; monoclinic `2` and `m` match Birss 1966 in the z-unique/first setting).
**So this is a documentation/UI-transparency item, not an engine bug.** Two
guard-rails from that session:
- **Forward order Z→Y→X:** `x ∥ a` and `y ∥ b*` are *consequences* of `Z∥c`,
  `Y∥(c×a)`, `X=Y×Z` together with `a⊥c` (monoclinic) — not independent
  assumptions. State the derivation in that order (the circular phrasing was
  flagged previously).
- **Anti-regression:** the app uses the **Birss first setting (z∥c)**, *not* the
  ITC b-unique second setting — do **not** "correct" `x∥a` to `x∥c` (a prior
  mistake). Triclinic was verified numerically for a general cell
  (α=80°, β=75°, γ=70°).

**Audit (v0.7.1, found in source).** Free [hkl] input is still present —
`hklInput` state + validation at `CalculatorPage.tsx:35, 42–48`, reset at `:272, :444`.
Presets are not yet reduced to Blickrichtungen: `PRESETS_BY_SYSTEM`
(`MathComponents.tsx:55–62`) gives **Cubic** = [001],[100],[010],[110],[111] (the
first three are equivalent ⟨100⟩ — the redundancy to drop → [100],[111],[110]) and
**Tetragonal** = [001],[100],[010],[110] ([010] redundant with [100] →
[001],[100],[110]). Hexagonal/Trigonal ([001],[100],[120]) and Mono/Triclinic
([001],[100],[010]∥b\*) are already as intended.

**Action items.**
- [ ] Remove the free [hkl] input (parsing, validation, UI).
- [ ] **Orthorhombic → cubic:** replace per-system presets with the (up to three)
      Blickrichtungen, one representative per ⟨…⟩ family; no symmetry-equivalent
      duplicates.
- [ ] Label presets crystallographically (e.g. b\* / [1̄20] for the hexagonal
      tertiary), consistent with `AxisOrientationInfo`.
- [ ] **Triclinic / monoclinic:** keep the existing conventional-axis presets; do
      **not** force a three-direction scheme. **Verify current
      `AxisOrientationInfo`** — later work may already return content for all
      seven systems, so confirm whether the *in-plane* anchor (x = projection of
      a onto ⊥c; y∥b\*) is actually stated, not just the z-axis; that specific
      anchor was the documented gap. Document the forward-order frame
      (Haussühl 1983 / IRE 1949: Z∥c, Y∥(c×a)∥b\*, X=Y×Z).

**Note — interaction with the v0.7-audit [hkl] items.** Removing free [hkl]
retires the "reachable on mobile" and "no malformed-input validation" items and
moots the non-cubic orthonormal-metric concern (cubic was the only place it
remained, and the orthonormal metric is correct there anyway). Azimuth-zero
anchoring then only needs to be defined for the fixed preset directions, not for
arbitrary [hkl].

## 3. Direction selection (cut / surface normal ∥ k)

_Choosing the cut / k direction, naming it clearly, and what depends on it._

### A1 — Calculator shows source terms as a function of angles that cannot be set there

**Status:** Decided for the Calculator (shipped); Simulator resolved (D4) — delete the φ_x, φ_y, ψ-symbolic block, keep "As functions of E_X,E_Y" and "As functions of θ_pol"; implementation pending.

**Area:** Calculator — Source Terms display
**Severity:** High — presents physically meaningless output *(provisional)*

**Problem.** The Calculator has **no angle inputs** — the lab-frame rotation /
polarization angles cannot be specified in this view at all. Yet the SHG source
terms are currently displayed as a function of those angles, which is meaningless
here. The Calculator evaluates one configuration for the selected propagation
direction **k** (cut/preset); the angles simply do not enter.

**Seen in v0.7.1 (screenshot, `6'mm'` › Source Terms, k∥[001]).** Confirmed and
stark: `S_X ∝ χ_xxx(−(3cos²φ_x cosφ_y cosψ sin²ψ + cos³φ_y cos³ψ + 6cosφ_x sinφ_x
cosφ_y sinφ_y cos²ψ sinψ − …))`, likewise `S_Y`, `S_Z` — full of `φ_x, φ_y, ψ`,
none of which exists as a control on this page. The lab-frame box even shows the
identity orientation (`x_crys = X_LAB`, …) for the selected k, so every angle is
effectively zero — yet the symbols remain in the display.

**Expected.** The Calculator shows the **angle-independent** source-term
expression for the selected k direction (cut/preset). The rotation/polarization
angles are not referenced in the display at all, because they cannot be set in
this view.

**Open point — Simulator (per request).** How the source terms should be
presented in the Simulator is deliberately left open. There the angle dependence
is intrinsic (polarimetry sweeps the polarization/sample angle), so a fixed-k
display is not obviously correct. Decide between:

- symbolic source terms **as a function of the swept angle** (cf. Feature 2,
  trig-polynomial representation), and/or
- per-angle evaluated values, vs.
- mirroring the Calculator's fixed-k behaviour.

Do **not** default to copying the Calculator behaviour into the Simulator.

**Action items.**
- [ ] Restrict the Calculator source-term display to the selected k direction.
- [ ] Resolve the Simulator presentation question (above) before implementing it
      there.

**To verify / locate.**
- The source-terms rendering path in the Calculator (Source Terms section / info
  header) — file/line to be filled in against the repo.
- Confirm which angle set is currently threaded into the display (lab-frame
  ψ/φ_x/φ_y vs. polarization angle).

### B8 — Unify and clearly name the direction selection (crystal surface normal ∥ k)

**Status:** Decided.

**Area:** Calculator (CUT + Source Terms) + Simulator — direction-selector naming & deduplication
**Severity:** Medium — UX clarity + mobile duplication *(provisional)*
**Relates to:** B5 (shared selector), B7 (presets/labels), A1 (angle-free source
term), A2 (tilt semantics).

**1 — Mobile Calculator shows the selector twice, differently named (but synced).**
On mobile the direction selector appears under **both** "CUT" (near the tensor
display) and "Source Terms (Lab Frame) → Select the direction of light
propagation…". The two button rows are **synchronized** (a choice in one
transfers to the other) but **labelled differently**. Deduplicate to a single
control with one name; if two anchor points must remain, give them the same label
and make the shared nature obvious.

**2 — Name the selection by what it physically is (user-facing model).**
What the user selects is the **crystal surface normal** — the crystallographic
direction [hkl] of the **cut** — which is placed **parallel to the lab beam
direction k**:

- **Calculator:** the normal is **∥ k** exactly (no tilt). This is the
  **reference orientation** the source term is computed for.
- **Simulator:** the normal can additionally be **tilted off k** via **φ_x, φ_y**
  (tilt about lab X/Y, cf. A2), with **ψ** spinning about the normal.

So the model is: *select cut normal [hkl] → it aligns with k → (Simulator only)
tilt it off k with φ_x/φ_y and spin with ψ.* The UI must state this plainly, so
"k ∥ [100]" reads as "cut with [100] as surface normal, aligned with the beam."

**Audit (v0.7.1).** The two synchronized Calculator selectors are `Cut`
(`CalculatorPage.tsx:263`) and the Source-Terms direction selector (`:414–447`);
both write the same orientation state (`thetaX/thetaY/psi0` + `hklInput`), which is
why selecting in one updates the other.

**Action items.**
- [ ] Deduplicate the mobile Calculator direction selector (one control, one

### B15 — Explorer as an interactive Birss table (tensor lookup up to rank 4)

**Status:** Open decision — scope & placement (larger vision item).

**Area:** Explorer (and/or a new "Tables" section) — general tensor lookup
**Severity:** Feature / vision — larger (roadmap-scale) scope *(exploratory)*
**Relates to:** the `manganite/birss-tables` repo (transcribed tables = golden
fixtures), B1 (grey-group c-tensors), B7/B8 (conventions), the SHG engine.

**Vision (Thomas).** Beyond SHG, make the Explorer an *interactive Birss table*:
look up arbitrary property tensors up to rank 4 for any (magnetic) point group,
the way one uses the printed Birss tables — preserving their **logic and
systematics**. SHG stays the headline application; this is the broader capability.

**The Birss systematics to preserve** (from the `birss-tables` repo — a
two-step, *factored* lookup):
1. **(point group, tensor type) → symbol class A–U.** Tensor type = rank (0–4) ×
   polar/axial × i-tensor (time-even) / c-tensor (time-odd). Table 4a maps the 32
   classical groups → class; Table 7 extends this to the magnetic groups (i- and
   c-tensor classes). Generating matrices: Table 3 (classical) / Table 6 (magnetic).
2. **(symbol class, rank) → tensor form.** Tables 4b–4f give the nonzero
   components, equalities and signs per class, indexed by rank (4b = r0 … 4f = r4),
   with Birss's permutation shorthand (`xz(2)`, `xxy(3)`, `xxxz(4)`, …).

The elegance: the 32/90 groups are **factored into ~21 classes (A–U)**, so forms
are tabulated once per class, not per group. (Grey/Type-II groups are omitted in
Tables 6/7: i-tensor = classical, c-tensor ≡ 0 — directly relevant to **B1**.)

**Feasibility.** The engine already generates tensor forms by projecting onto the
symmetry-invariant subspace; the approach generalizes to any rank ≤4 and type. So
most of the work is **specifying the tensor type + presenting results in Birss
form/notation**, not new physics. (Verify how rank-parametrized the current
generator is vs. hardcoded to the SHG case.) **Anti-circularity:** the interactive
output is engine-*generated*, so it must be validated against the transcribed
tables in `birss-tables` (golden fixtures) — never the reverse. Those markdown
tables are a ready-made automated-test fixture set.

**Where it lives — proposal (don't overload the Explorer): two entry points, one
engine.**
- **Explorer, per-group:** a lightweight "Tensor forms" tab — pick rank + type →
  show this group's **symbol class** and its **tensor form**. Group-centric, one
  extra tab, progressive disclosure, no clutter.
- **A dedicated "Tables" reference section (new sub-point):** mirrors the book —
  the A–U classes, Tables 4b–4f forms, the 4a/7 group→class mapping — for
  navigation by class/rank as in the book, cross-linked to the Explorer. Heavier
  reference content lives here, keeping the Explorer clean.

**Open questions.**
- Scope of tensor types exposed first (full rank 0–4 × polar/axial × i/c, or a
  curated subset?).
- Surface the **symbol class (A–U)** to users (it reveals which groups share a
  form)?
- Intrinsic index symmetries (Jahn-symbol / permutation symmetry of the property)
  — selectable, or rank+parity only at first?
- New top-level section vs. Explorer sub-tab vs. both.

**Action items.**
- [ ] Decide placement (Explorer sub-tab + dedicated "Tables" section, per above).
- [ ] Generalize the form generator to arbitrary rank ≤4 and type (polar/axial,
      i/c); confirm current rank-parametrization.
- [ ] Reproduce Birss notation (symbol classes A–U, permutation shorthand).
- [ ] Wire the `birss-tables` transcriptions in as golden-fixture validation.
- [ ] Cross-link group ↔ symbol class ↔ form.

### A2 — Simulator tilt axes φ_x, φ_y are crystal-fixed; should be lab-fixed (corrects Feature 1B roadmap spec)

**Status:** Derivation / verification pending — the corrected rotation composition must be re-derived and numerically re-checked.

**Area:** Simulator — three-angle sample rotation (Feature 1B)
**Severity:** Medium–High — wrong experimental geometry when ψ and tilts are combined *(provisional)*

**Problem.** The three-angle sample rotation currently couples the tilt axes to

  physically wrong for the intended goniometer geometry.

**Expected.** φ_x and φ_y must be tilts about the **fixed laboratory axes X and

crystal; spinning the sample (ψ) must not move them.

frame changes the parametrization, so the corrected composition must be:

- re-derived (operator order / which rotation is innermost vs. outermost), and
- re-checked numerically for the singularity-free (rank-3 at all presets)
  property — it must **not** silently reintroduce gimbal lock elsewhere.

- [ ] Re-derive the corrected rotation composition: φ_x, φ_y lab-fixed; ψ stays
      crystal-tied about the normal.
- [ ] Numerically verify rank 3 at all presets for the corrected composition

- [ ] Update the ROADMAP Feature 1B spec to the corrected frame convention.

**Open question — DEFERRED, still to be resolved.** The exact corrected

worked out and verified before Feature 1B is implemented / the ROADMAP spec is

### B16 — "Mathematical Model" box: simplify the trigonometric coefficients (and present them cleanly)

**Status:** Decided (D4) — drop the simultaneous 3-angle symbolic block; swept display variable is θ; harmonic form preferred, power form only when strictly shorter.

**Area:** Simulator — "Mathematical Model" box, Source Terms part (if it lives on
the Help page instead, same fix — cf. B14)
**Severity:** Medium — core math output is bloated/unreadable as presented *(provisional)*
**Relates to:** **Feature 2** (symbolic trig-polynomial representation of
polarimetry); B14 (Help math tab — same renderer); A1 (**distinct**: A1 is the
*Calculator* showing angle dependence it shouldn't — here the angle dependence is
correct, only the form is bloated).
**Reference:** Fröhlich et al., Appl. Phys. B **68**, 465 (1999) — eqs (3)/(6)/(9)
(YMnO₃, 6′mm′ — the physics the Simulator models). Add to the references (B14, tab 5).

**What's already fine.** The app **already factors by χ-component** and lists S_X,
S_Y separately. Only **one** tensor is ever shown (so no i-/c-tensor summation —
the earlier "separate blocks" idea is N/A).

**The actual problem — unsimplified trig coefficients.** Each χ-coefficient is a
long product-form trig polynomial in φ_x, φ_y, ψ, which is what makes the box
scroll sideways and read as a "catastrophe". Example (coefficient of E_X E_Y, from
the screenshot):

```
2 sinφ_x cos²φ_y cosψ sin²ψ − 2 sinφ_x cos²φ_y cos³ψ + 4 cosφ_x cos²φ_y sinφ_y cos²ψ sinψ
```

reduces (verified numerically to ~1e-15 over 2·10⁵ random angle triples) to:

```
cos²φ_y · [ −sinφ_x (cosψ + cos3ψ) + cosφ_x sinφ_y (sinψ + sin3ψ) ]
```

— a large compaction via product-to-sum / multiple-angle identities, and the
**harmonics cos3ψ / sin3ψ emerge**, which is physically meaningful (the n-fold
symmetry of the group surfaces as cos(nψ)/sin(nψ), cf. Fröhlich eq (6)'s
cos3φ_z′/sin3φ_z′).

**Layout (per Thomas).** No true column vector needed — **one line each for S_X and
S_Y** is fine. Keep the existing χ-factoring; just stop the horizontal scroll.

**Scope — do we need the full simultaneous 3-angle symbolic form? (recommendation:
no.)** The all-at-once symbolic dependence on φ_x, φ_y *and* ψ is the main source of
the blow-up and is rarely what the user needs. Instead:
- Substitute the **fixed orientation angles** (current slider values) numerically,
  and show symbolic dependence only on the angle that actually **varies in the
  current plot mode** — **θ** in every plot mode — θ_pol for Anisotropy and Polarizer, θ_ana for
  Analyzer (ψ and the tilts are one-time orientation inputs, never swept). That is exactly what is plotted, and it
  collapses to a short expression (Feature 2 territory).
- The fully general all-three-angle form: **not shown by default** — drop it, or
  offer it on demand / as a static reference in the Help derivation (B14), where a
  general derivation à la the paper is appropriate.

**Then** the trig simplification (above) makes the remaining one-variable
expression genuinely compact and exposes its harmonics.

**Keep — the numeric (orientation-substituted) forms are valuable.** With the
fixed orientation substituted, the χ-coefficients become numbers, and at **small
tilt** these nicely show **how new tensor components enter with small proportional
weights** (e.g. a `0.001·χ_xxz` term — a component just switching on) — the
Fröhlich insight that tilting accesses new components. So this is the form to
*feature*, not the general symbolic one. It is "somewhat cluttered" but the value
justifies keeping it; tidy rather than drop:
- drop redundant unit coefficients (`1·χ_zyy` → `χ_zyy`);
- hide or grey **exactly-zero** terms (`0·χ_zxx` is clutter; keep the
  small-but-nonzero ones like `0.001` — those are the point);
- control significant figures / apply a small display threshold.

**Action items.**
- [ ] One line each for S_X, S_Y (no column vector); kill the horizontal scroll.
- [ ] Feature the **numeric, orientation-substituted** form as a function of the
      **swept angle θ** (θ_pol for Anisotropy/Polarizer, θ_ana for Analyzer) — it shows the proportional
      onset of components with tilt; do not feature the simultaneous 3-angle form.
- [ ] Tidy the numeric form: drop `1·` coefficients, hide/grey exact-zero terms,
      control significant figures.
- [ ] Simplify any remaining one-variable trig to multiple-angle / harmonic form
      (Feature 2); verify numerically vs the raw form (e.g. the example above).
- [ ] (Optional) keep the fully general symbolic derivation only in the Help tab
      (B14) as static reference, not in the live box.
- [ ] Add Fröhlich et al. (1999) to the references.
